The science

What are cognitive distortions?

Cognitive distortions are irrational thought patterns that reinforce negative thinking. They were first identified by psychiatrist Aaron Beck in the 1960s as core features of depression and anxiety — and they show up in our language before we even notice them.

The problem

Mental health crises don't happen overnight

The average delay between the onset of mental health symptoms and receiving treatment is 11 years. By the time someone seeks help, patterns of distorted thinking have often become deeply entrenched.

CognitivePrint is built on a simple idea: if we can detect these patterns early — in the language people use every day — we can surface warning signs before they become a crisis.

What we detect

Four cognitive distortion patterns

Catastrophizing

Assuming the worst possible outcome. Treating setbacks as disasters.

"everything is ruined" · "it's hopeless" · "this is the worst"
Black & White Thinking

Seeing things in absolutes with no middle ground.

"I always fail" · "nobody cares" · "everything is wrong"
Mind Reading

Assuming you know what others think, usually negatively.

"they think I'm stupid" · "she hates me" · "nobody cares"
Self Blame

Taking personal responsibility for things outside your control.

"it's my fault" · "I ruined everything" · "because of me"
The pipeline

How the technology works

CognitivePrint runs a multi stage NLP pipeline on every journal entry, combining linguistic feature extraction, deep learning emotion classification, and graph theoretic pattern detection.

spaCyExtracts linguistic features — entities, POS tags, dependency parse trees
RoBERTaFine-tuned transformer model classifies emotion into 7 categories with confidence scoring
Distortion detectorRule-based lexical classifier flags 4 cognitive distortion patterns
NetworkXBuilds a time-series graph where each entry is a node connected to adjacent entries
BFS traversalDetects when distortion patterns cluster or worsen across consecutive entries
Health indexComposite 0–100 score: 60% distortion frequency + 40% negative sentiment rate
D3.jsForce-directed graph visualization with live node bloom animation
FlaskPython backend serving the NLP pipeline via REST API
